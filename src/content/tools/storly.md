Storly is inventory management built for small businesses that have outgrown spreadsheets but do not want the weight of a full ERP. It keeps the focus on inventory workflows rather than trying to be accounting software too, which makes it simpler to actually use.

The AI piece is practical rather than flashy. Storly surfaces insights on low stock, stock movement, and potential issues, so you spot problems faster instead of digging through rows yourself.

It is built for real shops. Workspace staff roles let an owner hand day-to-day stock management to staff without giving them access to billing or reports, so the right people see the right things.

The core features live together in one place: product tracking, categories, purchase orders, stock history, and reports. That is the toolkit a small seller actually needs, without the parts they don't.

It suits small shops, online sellers, boutiques, and repair stores. It is not the right fit for large enterprises needing complex ERP customization, warehouse automation, barcode hardware, advanced accounting, payroll, or multi-country compliance, and it is not built for service-only businesses with no physical stock to track.

Storly is still early, with the deliberate goal of giving smaller teams a cleaner way to track stock and cut spreadsheet work, not replacing heavyweight systems. There is a free tier to try it.
