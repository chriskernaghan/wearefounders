Leadpages is one of the longest-running landing page builders, and in 2026 it pivoted hard toward conversion rate optimisation. Unlimited traffic on every plan is the headline differentiator — most competitors cap your visitors and force upgrades when you scale.

## Who it's for

Founders running paid ads who need landing pages that aren't tied to their main marketing site. Course creators publishing sales pages. Affiliate marketers spinning up campaign-specific pages. Anyone whose landing page traffic spikes are unpredictable and who doesn't want to get stung by visitor overage fees.

If you only need one or two landing pages a year, the $99/mo entry price is hard to justify. Use Carrd or your existing site builder.

## What it actually does well

Unlimited traffic is the real value. Unbounce caps the equivalent plan at 20,000 visitors. Instapage caps at 15,000. Leadpages doesn't cap. For founders running cold traffic at scale, this removes a real cost category.

A/B testing is included from the Grow tier. Most competitors gate testing behind a $149-249/mo plan. If you're optimising conversion rates seriously, this saves an upgrade tier.

The drag-and-drop builder is genuinely easy to learn. Most founders are publishing their first page within an hour. Templates are conversion-focused rather than design-focused, which is the right call for the use case.

AI page creation is included on every plan. Type a prompt, get a draft page. Useful as a starting point, not a finished product.

## Where it gets awkward

The 2025 pricing change pushed the entry tier from $49/mo to $99/mo (or $79/mo annual). For founders who remember Leadpages as the affordable option, the new pricing feels less competitive against Landingi (free plan) or older Leadpages plans.

No free plan, just a 7-day trial. Aggressive compared to Landingi's free tier or Webflow's free Starter.

Smart Traffic (AI visitor routing) and heatmaps require Optimize at $199/mo. Doubling the spend for what most competitors call "advanced features" is a meaningful jump.

## How it compares

Versus Unbounce: Unbounce has visitor caps but better Smart Traffic AI and a longer optimization track record. Pick Leadpages if traffic volume is unpredictable, Unbounce if you want serious AI-driven testing.

Versus Webflow: Webflow gives you a full site builder, Leadpages gives you a landing page specialist. Leadpages publishes faster, Webflow scales further.

Versus Carrd: Carrd is dramatically cheaper and fine for one-page sites. Leadpages makes sense once you need real testing, integrations, and multiple campaigns.
