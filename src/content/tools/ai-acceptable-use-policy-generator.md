The AI Acceptable Use Policy Generator gives teams a practical first draft of an AI usage policy in about a minute. You fill in a few details about your organization, and it produces a 10-section policy covering approved tools, data handling, human review, prohibited uses, incident reporting, and review cadence. Everything runs in the browser, so nothing is transmitted to or stored by TinyOps.

The output is deliberately framed as a starting point rather than a finished document. It flags decisions that still need sign-off and is explicit that it isn't legal advice, a security audit, or a compliance certification, so you know to route it through the right people before adoption.

For teams that want more than the free draft, a $79 template pack adds a tool register, rollout checklist, and implementation guide, and TinyOps offers fixed-scope customization for $499. There's also a free companion open governance kit on GitHub for teams that want to go further on their own.
