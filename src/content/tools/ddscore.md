DDScore gives founders an investor-style first pass on their pitch deck and supporting materials before the real thing. It cross-checks claims in the deck against each other and against current public sources, then scores the business across 12 due diligence dimensions, business idea, offering, team, market, competitors, technology and IP, scalability, legal and regulatory, exit, presentation, financial critique, and fundability, each with confidence, same-stage benchmarking, strengths, risks, and evidence gaps.

Because a founder can re-run the same company as materials evolve, DDScore works as a fix-and-recheck loop rather than a one-off grade. Uploaded materials are processed in Finland, never used for model training, deleted once the report generates, and the report itself is deleted within 24 hours.

**What it does differently:**
- Cross-checks claims in pitch decks and supporting documents against one another and current public sources in a single 10-30 minute first pass
- Produces a comparable 0-100 score across 12 due diligence dimensions, with confidence, same-stage benchmarking, strengths, risks, evidence gaps, and unresolved questions
- Supports repeat analysis of the same company, so founders can address gaps before investor review
- Processes materials in Finland, never uses them for model training, deletes uploads when the report is generated, and deletes the report within 24 hours

**Not a good fit for:** stock picking, investment advice, automated investment decisions, or a substitute for full legal, financial, commercial, or technical due diligence. It's not built for public-equity trading or real-time market signals, it works best when a founder, investor, or evaluator has meaningful private-company materials and wants a structured first pass before deeper human review.

DDScore is a live product by Playful Pixels Oy in Espoo, Finland, with roughly four years of product development behind it. A free trial includes one summary report; the Founder Package is €39 one-time for up to five re-runs on the same company.
