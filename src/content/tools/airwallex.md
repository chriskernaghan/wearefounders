If you're billing customers in one currency, paying contractors in another, and banking in a third, traditional business banking taxes every move. Airwallex fixes most of that with a single account that holds 20+ currencies natively.

## Who it's for

Founders running operations across borders. SaaS companies with international customers, agencies with overseas contractors, e-commerce brands sourcing from Asia. Pre-launch UK and EU founders also use it as a primary account.

If your operations are entirely domestic, the case is weaker. Mercury (US) or Tide (UK) probably suit you better.

## What it actually does well

Holding currencies natively is the differentiator most founders underrate until they need it. When a US customer pays you in dollars, those dollars sit in a USD balance. When you pay a Polish contractor in zloty, you draw from a PLN balance you topped up at a competitive FX rate. No forced conversion at every transaction.

The card programme is solid: virtual cards for subscriptions, physical cards for travel and ad spend, expense management built in. Invoicing handles international tax requirements.

## Where it gets awkward

KYC onboarding takes longer than challenger banks built around speed. Expect document requests and verification calls, especially if you're a newer entity. US availability is narrower than UK, EU, and APAC, and some advanced treasury features are locked behind volume tiers.

## How it compares

Versus Wise Business: Airwallex is more of a full account, Wise is more of a transfer tool with account features attached. For founders who need actual banking infrastructure, Airwallex pulls ahead.

Versus Revolut Business: similar feature set, different geographies of strength. Airwallex is stronger in APAC, Revolut is stronger in EU consumer-facing tools.
