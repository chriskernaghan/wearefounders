CueVault is a smarter take on YouTube's Watch Later. The real strength is retrieval: AI semantic search lets you come back and find exactly what you need by context, meaning, or concept, without having to remember the video title or the right keywords. You type what it was about, and it surfaces the clip.

Capturing is just as frictionless. You save the good bits from inside the player as you watch, so you never leave full screen or break the flow of the video to jot something down.

AI titling and summaries handle the writing for you. Instead of pausing to label each bookmark yourself, CueVault generates the title and summary, so your attention stays on the content.

It takes a local-first approach. Your cues live on your own machine and are stored in a database only to power your AI-driven searches, which keeps your library yours.

CueVault is built for people who watch longer-format YouTube and want to actually retrieve the value later. If you mostly consume Shorts, it is not the right fit. Paid plans start at 5.99 dollars a month, and there is a free tier to try it first.
